Africa Humanitarian Action is a pan-African international non-governmental organization (INGO) that provides effective relief, development and advocacy services. Founded in 1994 in response to the atrocities in Rwanda, AHA has supported over 12 million people in 17 African countries to regain their health, dignity and wellbeing. At the heart of AHA’s work is a firm commitment to reducing poverty through addressing crisis-related suffering and laying the foundations for recovery and sustainable development. AHA works in partnership with local populations, listening and responding to people’s needs and empowering them to make lasting differences to their own lives. AHA believes that it is only through building on the capacities existent within communities and giving people ownership of their future, that sustainable change can be achieved.

Complementary to the assistance AHA provides is its work in international advocacy, bringing a much needed Southern perspective to the global humanitarian arena. Since inception, AHA has striven to empower African civil society organizations and communities to ensure that they have the opportunity, confidence and space to generate meaningful contributions to humanitarianism. The strength of AHA is in its African roots and reach. It attempts to deliver sound programs and results. AHA is inclusive and makes use of the diversity and resources of the people it works with. AHA embraces change and innovation, at the same time as it seeks results that last.
